Course contents

This course focuses on the components comprising a network, as well as the ways in which these components cooperate to accomplish data communication. 'Components' are understood to include hardware and software. In most cases specific products will not be considered, but rather the functions that are expected from each component.

It will become clear very early that a network consists of a very large number of components. It is very important to get to know the names of these components (that often consist of acronyms or reference numbers) and to place them in context. The OSI ISO reference model covered in chapter 2 of the textbook forms the key that should be used to place components in context. It forms the basis of this course.

Due to the fact that it is important that products from various vendors can be linked in a network, the use of standards for data communications is even more important than its use in other branches of information technology. A fair amount of time will therefore be spent to study relevant standards.

The practical component of the course is intended to give the student a feeling of the work (and frustration!) when one develops communicating programs. The intention behind most of the practical assignments is direct contact with network protocols. The student will therefore be expected to implement these (application layer) protocols, rather than to use pre-existing classes that already implement such protocols. Note that, if our primary intention were software development, the use of such classes would have been encouraged. However, the primary intention here is exploration of the operation of the protocols concerned.

The semester test and examination will include aspects of practical assignments up to the date of the examination or test.

Practical

Practical assignments will be available before the due date on the course site.

Practical periods will normally only be used for demonstration purposes - students are allowed to demonstrate their projects any time during the practical period. Practical assignments have to be uploaded to the departmental server before the demonstrations. A due date and time by which each assignment has to uploaded will be specified. An automatic grace period of 24 hours after that time will be allowed, but no uploads will be allowed after the grace period has expired. Only programs that have been uploaded will be allowed during the demonstration session. (And programs that are found to be evidently similar will be disqualified from the demonstration session - or disqualified afterwards.) Upload a zipped or tgz archive that contains the source code and an executable image of your program. Since programs will be marked during the demonstration sessions, only programs that have been demonstrated will earn any marks.

Composition of semester mark

  • Semester test 1: 25%
  • Semester test 2: 35%
  • Class tests: 10%
  • Practical demonstrations: 30%

Class tests will be written during the lecture on Mondays. The idea is to give you an indication of your progress acquiring the knowledge required to pass this course, as well as to give you an indication of the level of detail at which you need to know the material.

The semester mark and examination mark will carry equal weights when the final mark is calculated. A final mark of 50% is required to pass. Note that a subminimum of 40% is required during the semester to be allowed to write the examination. A subminimum of 40% in the examination is required to pass.

Integrity and ethics

As someone in the process of becoming an IT professional, you will be expected to act professionally at all times. This includes acting in a manner as described in the codes of conduct of professional bodies, such as the Computer Society of South Africa and the ACM. In particular, it implies that you will never present someone else's work as your own. Use of others' work is only permitted to the extent that such use complies with intellectual property laws. Specifically, you are expected to know what plagiarism is, and to take active steps to avoid plagiarism. Any assignment that is found to contain portions of plagiarised text, code, or other material, will be awarded 0 and further disciplinary steps may be taken. See the UP website on this topic for further details.

Absence from tests

When a test is not written due to ill health or another valid reason, you have to apply in writing to write a sick test. When practical assignments are handed in late for similar reasons, it is again necessary to apply in writing. Supporting documentation has to be attached.
